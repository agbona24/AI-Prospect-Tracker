## The Lagos Web Design Market in 2026

Lagos is not one market — it's five distinct markets layered on top of each other. Understanding this changes how you prospect and price.

| Zone | Business character | Typical web design budget |
|------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Victoria Island / Ikoyi** | Corporate, professional services, multinationals | ₦400,000 – ₦2,000,000+ |
| **Lekki Phase 1 / 2** | SMEs, upscale restaurants, boutiques, clinics | ₦250,000 – ₦700,000 |
| **Ikeja / GRA** | Mixed — corporate offices, established SMEs | ₦200,000 – ₦600,000 |
| **Surulere / Yaba** | Tech startups, creative businesses, mid-market | ₦150,000 – ₦400,000 |

Start your prospecting in Lekki, Ikeja, and VI. These areas have businesses with genuine marketing budgets and owners who understand the value of a website.

## Top Industries in Lagos With Untapped Website Demand

### 1. Law Firms
Lagos has the highest concentration of law firms in Nigeria. Most mid-sized firms — not the Templars and SimmonsCooper giants, but the two-to-ten partner practices in Ikeja, VI, and Lekki — have outdated websites or none at all.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- High billing rates mean they can invest in quality
- Websites drive real enquiries (people Google "solicitor Lagos" before calling)
- They respond to professional outreach

**Typical project value**: ₦400,000 – ₦800,000

---

### 2. Private Clinics and Dental Practices
Healthcare is booming in Lagos. Private clinics, specialist practices, and dental surgeries have opened in massive numbers since 2020 — and most still rely on word of mouth and Instagram alone.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- Patients search online for clinics before booking
- A well-designed site with an appointment booking feature generates immediate ROI
- Clinic owners are educated and understand investment

**Typical project value**: ₦300,000 – ₦600,000

---

### 3. Hotels and Short-Stay Apartments
Lagos has hundreds of hotels, boutique guesthouses, and serviced apartments competing for the same guests. The difference between a ₦15,000/night room and a ₦35,000/night room is often just digital presence.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- A direct booking system saves them 15–20% OTA commission
- They have ongoing content needs (events, promotions)
- The ROI from one extra direct booking per day justifies the entire site cost

**Typical project value**: ₦350,000 – ₦800,000 + ₦30,000–₦60,000/month retainer

---

### 4. Private Schools and Tutoring Centres
Competition for enrolment in Lagos private schools is fierce. Parents Google schools before visiting. Schools that appear professional and credible online win enrolments at a higher rate.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- Long-term clients — they need content updates every term
- Decision-maker is usually the proprietor (fast decisions)
- Strong ROI: one extra enrolment at ₦500,000/year fees = 2x the website cost

**Typical project value**: ₦200,000 – ₦450,000

---

### 5. Real Estate Agencies
Lagos is one of the most active property markets in Africa. Real estate agencies that don't have a proper listings site lose buyers and renters to competitors who do.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- High-value transactions mean they can invest heavily
- Websites need regular updates (listings) → natural retainer relationship
- International clients (diaspora) specifically rely on websites to shortlist properties

**Typical project value**: ₦500,000 – ₦1,500,000

## Pricing for Lagos Clients

Lagos clients — especially in VI, Lekki, and Ikeja — expect professional pricing. If you quote ₦50,000, they will question your quality. If you quote ₦250,000 confidently and can back it up with portfolio work, they respect it.

**Minimum pricing for Lagos (2026):**

| Package | Price | Scope |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Starter | ₦150,000 – ₦250,000 | 5 pages, mobile, WhatsApp button |
| Business | ₦300,000 – ₦600,000 | 8–12 pages, booking system, SEO |
| E-commerce | ₦700,000 – ₦1,500,000 | Full store with Paystack |
| Monthly maintenance | ₦25,000 – ₦80,000/month | All above |

Never discount in the first conversation. Discounting signals that your original price was inflated. If a client pushes back, offer to reduce scope — not price.
