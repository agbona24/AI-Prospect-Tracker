The best free lead generation tool depends on how long you need "free" to last. Apollo.io's free tier gives you the biggest database (210M+ contacts) but burns through credits fast. Hunter.io's free plan caps at 25 searches a month — fine for occasional lookups, thin for active prospecting. Snov.io gives 50 free credits monthly with basic sequencing attached. Runvax is the only one built to stay free indefinitely for its core use case — finding local businesses with no website — because it isn't rationing access to a finite contact database, it's querying live business listings.

None of these are "the best" outright. Here's what each free tier actually gets you before you hit a paywall.

## Why "Free" Tiers All Have a Catch

Free tiers exist to get you hooked on a workflow, then charge you once you depend on it. That's not a criticism — it's just how credit-based SaaS pricing works. The catch is almost always volume: Apollo, Hunter, and Snov.io all give you real functionality for free, but ration it in credits or searches that a genuinely active prospecting habit burns through in days, not months.

Runvax's free plan works differently because the constraint isn't a shrinking credit pool — it's a daily search cap that resets every day rather than depleting permanently. That matters if you're prospecting consistently rather than in short bursts.

## Apollo.io Free Tier

Apollo's free plan is genuinely generous on paper — full access to its contact database with title, seniority, and industry filters. The limitation is credits: you get a capped number of email/phone reveals per month, and any real prospecting workflow (searching 50+ contacts a week) exhausts that fast. It's a strong free option if your search volume is low and your targets are companies with an existing web/LinkedIn presence. For a deeper look at where Apollo fits versus lighter tools, see [Best Apollo.io Alternatives in 2026](/blog/apollo-io-alternatives).

## Hunter.io Free Tier

25 searches a month works out to roughly one search a day — usable if you're verifying a handful of emails a week, not building a prospecting pipeline. Hunter doesn't ration by "credits" in the confusing sense Apollo does; it's a flat search count, which makes it easier to track when you'll hit the wall. It's the simplest free tier to understand, just also the smallest.

## Snov.io Free Tier

50 credits a month sits between Hunter and Apollo in generosity, and Snov bundles in basic drip campaign functionality that neither Hunter nor Apollo's free tier includes. If you want email finding and simple follow-up sequences in one free plan, Snov.io's tier is the most functionally complete of the three database tools — though the credit ceiling is still tight for daily use.

## Runvax Free Plan

Runvax's free plan gives 5 searches a day at 20 results each — roughly 100 fresh local business leads daily, with the no-website flag included on every result, no card required. AI-generated outreach (email, WhatsApp message, or proposal) is available on the free plan too, not gated behind a paywall the way sequencing features are on the other tools. The tradeoff is scope: Runvax's free tier is deep for local SMB discovery and shallow for anything else — it's not trying to be a general contact database.

## Free Non-Tool Methods Worth Knowing

Before any tool, the oldest free method is still real: manually browsing Google Maps or Facebook Business pages for listings with no website link, or checking industry directories that list phone numbers without a URL. It works, it's genuinely free forever, and it's slow — 2-3 minutes per business checked by hand versus seconds per result in a tool built to flag it automatically. Worth doing for your first 10-20 leads to understand the pattern; not worth doing at volume once you're prospecting seriously.
