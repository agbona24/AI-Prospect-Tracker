The best Apollo.io alternative depends on what you're actually trying to do with it: Hunter.io if you just need email finding without the sequencing overhead, Snov.io if price is the main objection, and Runvax if your target market is local small businesses rather than enterprise B2B contacts — a segment Apollo's database barely covers.

Apollo is a genuinely strong enterprise sales tool. People look for alternatives for one of three reasons: it's more than they need and costs more than they want to pay, its database coverage thins out for local/regional small businesses (especially outside the US, UK, and Europe), or they just need a lighter, cheaper tool for a narrower job.

## Why People Look for Apollo Alternatives

Apollo's pricing scales with team size and usage — $59-149/month per user depending on tier — which is real money for a solo freelancer or two-person agency. The bigger structural issue for some users is coverage: Apollo's database is built from LinkedIn profiles, company websites, and B2B directories, which is excellent for the US, UK, and Europe but has real gaps in markets like Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya, and other regions where small businesses run on Google Maps and WhatsApp rather than LinkedIn and corporate websites.

If you've searched Apollo for restaurants, salons, or clinics in a specific city and gotten thin or outdated results, that's the database limitation showing — not a settings problem.

## Hunter.io: The Leaner Alternative

If Apollo feels like more tool than you need — you just want to find and verify an email address without a full sequencing suite — Hunter is the direct downgrade path. It's cheaper, faster to use for a single lookup, and doesn't try to be a full sales platform. It shares Apollo's core limitation for local businesses without a website, though, since both depend on there being a domain to search.

## Snov.io: The Budget Alternative

Snov.io undercuts Apollo on price (~$39/mo vs ~$59/mo entry) while covering similar ground — email finding plus basic drip campaigns. It's a reasonable landing spot if cost is the primary driver and you're doing standard B2B prospecting rather than local SMB outreach.

## Runvax: The Alternative for Local Business Leads

If your actual complaint about Apollo is "it doesn't have the businesses I'm trying to reach," switching to Hunter or Snov.io won't fix that — they're built on the same category of data. Runvax is a structurally different tool: it searches live business listing data by city and category, which is where local businesses (with or without websites) actually show up, regardless of whether they have a corporate domain or LinkedIn page.
