Cold email is the most reliable way to get web design clients — when done right.

The problem is most web designers send emails that sound like everyone else. Generic subject lines. Long paragraphs about "digital presence." No specific reason why you're reaching out to *that* business.

Here are 7 templates that work. Each one is short, specific, and easy to personalise.

---

## Before You Use These Templates

One thing makes cold email dramatically more effective: knowing the business has **no website before you reach out**.

When you email a business that already has a website, you're pitching against their current provider. When you email one with *no* website, you're the only option on the table.

[Runvax](https://runvax.com) lets you search any city and industry and flags which businesses have no website. That's where to focus your outreach.

## Template 1: The Direct Opener (Best Overall)

**Subject: Quick question, [Business Name]**

> Hi [Owner/Manager Name],
>
> I was searching for [business type] in [City] and came across [Business Name].
>
> I noticed you don't have a website yet — and I build professional sites for businesses like yours. Usually live within 7 days.
>
> Would it be useful to see what a site for [Business Name] could look like? I can send a free mockup.
>
> [Your Name]
> [Phone Number]

**Why it works:** It's specific (mentions their business name and city), identifies the pain (no website), and offers value upfront (free mockup) rather than asking them to commit.

---

## Template 2: The Revenue Angle

**Subject: Customers are searching for you online — and not finding you**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I searched "[business type] in [City]" on Google and [Business Name] didn't come up — because you don't have a website yet.
>
> That means potential customers looking for exactly what you offer are going to your competitors instead.
>
> I help local businesses like yours get online fast. A professional website with your menu/services, contact details, and location — typically ready in under 2 weeks.
>
> Worth a quick chat?
>
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** Frames the problem as lost revenue (money already being lost) rather than an abstract "digital presence" pitch.

---

## Template 3: The Short & Punchy

**Subject: [Business Name] — website?**

> Hi,
>
> I build websites for [business type]s in [City].
>
> Noticed [Business Name] doesn't have one yet. Happy to send you a sample layout if you're curious.
>
> [Your Name]
> [Your number]

**Why it works:** Extremely short. Business owners are busy. This respects their time and still lands the key point.

---

## Template 4: The Referral Opener (for warm leads)

**Subject: Built [Similar Business]'s site — wanted to reach out**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I recently built the website for [Similar Local Business] — a [business type] also based in [City].
>
> I noticed [Business Name] doesn't have a website yet and thought you might find it useful. Happy to show you what I built for them as a reference.
>
> Would you be open to a quick call this week?
>
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** Social proof from a local, recognisable business builds instant trust. Use this after your first client in an area or industry.

## Template 5: The Clinic/Medical Pitch

**Subject: Patients are looking for your clinic online**

> Hi Dr. [Name] / Hi [Clinic Name] team,
>
> I searched for clinics in [Area] and noticed [Clinic Name] doesn't have a website yet.
>
> Most patients now Google a clinic before they visit — checking hours, location, services, and whether they take their insurance/HMO.
>
> I build professional healthcare websites that help clinics get found online and reduce front-desk calls. Happy to show you a sample.
>
> Best,
> [Your Name]

**Why it works:** Medical practices have specific patient behaviour you can reference. Speaks to the owner's actual pain (front-desk calls, missed patients).

## Template 6: The Restaurant Pitch

**Subject: Is [Restaurant Name] on Google yet?**

> Hi,
>
> I searched for [cuisine type] restaurants in [City] and found [Restaurant Name] — but noticed you don't have a website showing your menu.
>
> Most diners check a restaurant's menu online before deciding where to go. If you're not showing up, you're losing tables to competitors who are.
>
> I build restaurant websites with full menus, reservation forms, and Google Maps integration — usually ready in 5–7 days.
>
> Want me to send a sample layout for [Restaurant Name]?
>
> [Your Name]

## Template 7: The WhatsApp Follow-Up

Send this 3–4 days after the email if you haven't heard back:

> Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name], a web designer. I sent you an email last week about building a website for [Business Name]. Just following up in case it got lost.
>
> I can show you a free sample design of what your site could look like — no commitment needed. Would that be useful?

**WhatsApp rules:**
- Keep it under 3 sentences
- Don't use voice notes for first contact
- Send between 9am–5pm local time
- Don't follow up more than twice
