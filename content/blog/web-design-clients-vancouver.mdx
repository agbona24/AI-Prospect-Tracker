Vancouver has a dense, highly independent small-business economy — and a large share of the businesses outside downtown's core commercial strips still don't have a real website. The fastest way to find them is targeted search by neighbourhood and trade, not scrolling social media hoping to spot a gap.

If you design websites in or around Metro Vancouver, here's where to focus, which industries convert best, and how to build a repeatable weekly pipeline.

---

## The Vancouver Market in 2026

Vancouver's business geography breaks into distinct commercial pockets, each with a different budget profile and client type.

| Area | Business character | Typical website budget |
|------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Downtown / Coal Harbour** | Finance, law, corporate professional services | CAD $4,000 – $15,000+ |
| **Yaletown** | Tech, agencies, upscale retail and hospitality | CAD $2,000 – $6,000 |
| **Gastown** | Creative studios, boutique retail, hospitality | CAD $1,500 – $4,500 |
| **Kitsilano** | Wellness, fitness, independent retail | CAD $800 – $2,500 |
| **Mount Pleasant / Main Street** | Independent shops, cafés, creative businesses | CAD $700 – $2,200 |
| **Surrey / Burnaby / Richmond** | Trades, family-run retail, community services | CAD $600 – $2,000 |

Downtown and Yaletown carry the biggest single-project budgets, but Surrey, Burnaby, and Mount Pleasant have the largest raw number of independent businesses with no website at all.

## Top Industries in Vancouver With Untapped Website Demand

### 1. Independent Trades and Contractors
Metro Vancouver's trades economy is large and largely undocumented online — most sole proprietors and small firms rely on word of mouth and a Facebook page rather than a website they own.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- A simple quote-request site converts fast with almost no online competition
- Strong, consistent cash flow to invest in their business
- Fast decisions — the tradesperson is usually reading your outreach directly

**Typical project value**: CAD $800 – $2,000

---

### 2. Wellness, Fitness, and Yoga Studios
Kitsilano and Mount Pleasant have some of the highest concentrations of independent wellness and fitness studios in Canada, many running on Instagram and a booking app alone, with no real website.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- Clients research and book classes online before ever visiting
- Booking-integration sites deliver immediate, measurable value
- Strong visual brands make for excellent portfolio work

**Typical project value**: CAD $1,000 – $3,000

---

### 3. Independent Cafés and Restaurants
Gastown and Main Street alone have dozens of independent food and drink venues that rely entirely on Instagram presence.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- A focused one-page site (menu, hours, reservations) is a fast, affordable build
- High visual appeal — great portfolio material
- Tight hospitality networks drive fast referrals

**Typical project value**: CAD $700 – $2,200

---

### 4. Real Estate and Property Services
Vancouver's property market is among the most active in North America, and many independent brokers and small property management firms still rely on MLS listings alone.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- High transaction values justify serious investment
- Ongoing listing updates create a natural maintenance retainer
- Local SEO ("realtor Burnaby") is genuinely winnable for independents

**Typical project value**: CAD $2,000 – $5,000

---

### 5. Import/Export and Trade Businesses
Vancouver's port economy supports a large number of small import/export and logistics firms, particularly in Richmond, many of which operate with no website beyond a business directory listing.

**Why they're ideal clients:**
- B2B buyers expect to vet suppliers online before contacting them
- Multilingual site needs (English/Mandarin/Punjabi) create ongoing retainer work
- Clear, easy ROI story around credibility with new buyers

**Typical project value**: CAD $1,500 – $4,000

## Writing Cold Outreach That Works in Vancouver

Vancouver business owners respond well to outreach that's specific and low-pressure — generic sales language gets ignored fast in this market.

**Cold email template:**

> **Subject: [Business Name] — quick website idea**
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I build websites for [trade/studios/independent restaurants] around [area], and noticed [Business Name] doesn't have one yet.
>
> Most people searching for [service] near [area] pick whoever they find online first — right now that's not you.
>
> I can put together a free concept of what your site could look like, no obligation. Interested?
>
> [Your name]

**Follow-up (3-4 days later):**

> "Hi [Name], just following up on the concept idea — happy to send it over this week if useful, no pressure either way."
