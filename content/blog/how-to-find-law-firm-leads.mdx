Law firms are one of the highest-paying niches in web design — and one of the most underserved. Reputation is everything in legal services, and a dated or missing website actively costs firms new clients who research online before calling.

---

## Why Law Firms Are Worth Targeting

- **Higher budgets.** Legal services command premium fees, and firms are used to paying for quality — a professional website is a rounding error against their revenue.
- **Trust sells the service.** Prospective clients research attorneys online before calling. A firm with no website — or an outdated one — looks less credible than competitors, even if they're just as good.
- **Low competition per city.** Unlike restaurants or salons, there are fewer law firms per neighborhood, so you can realistically research and personalize outreach to every one.
- **Long-term client relationships.** Law firms rarely switch web vendors once they trust one — a single win here can mean years of maintenance and update work.

---

## Step 1: Search Law Firms by City and Practice Area

In [ProspectAI](https://prospectai.beamai.net), search "law firm" or a specific practice area — "family lawyer," "criminal defense attorney," "immigration lawyer" — for your target city. Each result is flagged for website presence.

Practice-area searches often surface solo practitioners who are more likely to still be operating without a website than large multi-partner firms.

---

## Step 2: Prioritize Firms With No Website or an Outdated One

A missing website is the clearest signal. But for this niche, also flag firms whose sites look outdated (no HTTPS, non-mobile-friendly, stock template design) — they're just as winnable, and there are usually more of them than firms with zero web presence.

---

## Step 3: Lead With Credibility, Not Price

Law firm pitches should never open with a discount — it undercuts the professionalism angle you're selling. Instead:

> **Subject: [Firm Name]'s online presence**
>
> Hi [Attorney Name],
>
> I was researching [practice area] attorneys in [City] and noticed [Firm Name] doesn't currently have a website — while several competitors do, and rank ahead of you in search results.
>
> I design professional websites specifically for law firms — built to establish credibility and convert visitors into consultation requests.
>
> Would you be open to a short call, or would you like to see a free mockup first?
>
> [Your Name]

ProspectAI generates a personalized version of this outreach for every firm in your results.

---

## Step 4: Emphasize Compliance and Professionalism in the Design

Law firm websites need to look conservative and trustworthy, not flashy. When you follow up with a mockup, lean into:

- Clean, formal typography and a muted color palette
- Attorney bios and credentials prominently displayed
- Clear practice-area pages (not a single generic "services" page)
- A visible consultation request form or phone number

---

## Step 5: Price at a Premium

| Package | Includes |
|---|---|
| Solo practitioner | 5–7 pages, attorney bio, practice areas, contact form |
| Small firm | 10+ pages, multiple attorney profiles, case results, blog |

Law firms are one of the few local-business niches where charging a premium (rather than the lowest price) actually improves your close rate — it signals quality.
