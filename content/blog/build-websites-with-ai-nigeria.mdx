## The AI Web Design Workflow That Works in Nigeria

Here's the complete process — from finding the client to delivering a finished website — using AI at every stage.

---

### Stage 1: Find the Client (10 Minutes)

Before you build anything, you need a paying client.

[Runvax](https://runvax.com) searches any Nigerian city by industry and identifies businesses with no website. Search "restaurants Lekki" or "clinics Abuja" and you get a list of businesses — with each one flagged as having a website or not.

No-website businesses are your warmest leads. They have an obvious problem. You have the solution.

Once you find a prospect, Runvax generates a personalised outreach message automatically. Send it. Wait for a reply. Book a discovery call.

**Time: 10–15 minutes per day to maintain a full pipeline.**

---

### Stage 2: Discovery Call (30 Minutes)

Before you touch a design tool, understand the client's business.

Questions to ask:
1. "What does your business do and who are your main customers?"
2. "What do you want visitors to do when they land on your site?" (call, book, buy, enquire)
3. "Do you have any examples of sites you like?"
4. "What's your timeline? Any specific deadline?"
5. "Do you have existing branding — logo, colours, fonts?"
6. "Do you want a blog? A booking system? WhatsApp button?"

Write the answers down. These become your brief to the AI tools in the next stage.

---

### Stage 3: Build With AI Tools (2–8 Hours)

Choose your tool based on the project type:

---

#### For Business Websites (Most Common)

**Tool: Lovable + Claude**

1. Open [Lovable](https://lovable.dev) and create a new project
2. Write a detailed prompt:

> "Build a professional website for Lagos Dental Clinic, a dental practice in Lekki, Lagos Nigeria. Pages needed: Home, Services (list: general dentistry, teeth whitening, braces, root canal), About Us (Dr. Adaeze Okonkwo has 15 years experience), Gallery, Contact with a WhatsApp booking button to 08012345678. The colour scheme should be clean and medical — white, navy blue, and light teal. Include a Google Maps embed for their Lekki Phase 1 address. Mobile responsive."

3. Lovable builds a working multi-page site in minutes
4. Review what it built and refine with follow-up prompts:
   - "Change the hero section to show a smiling patient and dentist in the background"
   - "Make the WhatsApp button green and sticky on mobile"
   - "Add a section for patient testimonials"

5. For any code issues, paste the problematic section into Claude:
   - "This CSS isn't making the navigation mobile-responsive. Fix it."
   - Claude explains and fixes the issue

**Delivery time: 2–4 hours for a complete 5-page site**

---

#### For E-Commerce Sites

**Tool: Lovable + Paystack Integration**

Nigerian e-commerce needs Paystack or Flutterwave for payments. Lovable can integrate these.

Prompt example:

> "Build an online store for Amaka's Fashion House, a Lagos clothing boutique. Products: Ankara dresses (₦15,000–₦45,000), men's agbada sets (₦25,000–₦80,000), accessories. Integrate Paystack for payment. Include product categories, a shopping cart, and order confirmation emails. Brand colours: gold and deep maroon."

For complex e-commerce with inventory management, use Claude to build on top of a Lovable base or switch to a WordPress + WooCommerce + Paystack stack.

**Delivery time: 1–2 days for a full e-commerce site**

---

#### For Design-Forward / Portfolio Sites

**Tool: Framer**

For architecture firms, photographers, creative agencies, fashion brands — clients who care deeply about visual presentation — Framer gives you design control that Lovable doesn't.

1. Choose a Framer template closest to the client's industry
2. Replace all content (text, images) with the client's brand
3. Use Framer AI to generate page sections: "Add a project case study section with before/after images"
4. Publish directly on Framer (you can add a custom domain the client owns)

**Delivery time: 1–2 days for a polished design-forward site**

---

### Stage 4: Generate Images With AI (30 Minutes)

Most Nigerian businesses don't have professional photography. Instead of telling clients they need a photoshoot (which delays the project), use AI-generated images as placeholders — or as final images if the client approves.

**Using Midjourney:**

> "Professional interior photo of a modern dental clinic in Lagos, Nigeria, bright natural lighting, Nigerian dentist in a white coat examining a patient, clean and clinical aesthetic, wide shot --ar 16:9 --v 6"

> "Exterior photo of a boutique hotel in Lekki Lagos at golden hour, luxury vehicles parked outside, warm ambient lighting, architectural photography --ar 16:9 --v 6"

Generate 5–10 images per section, pick the best ones. Clients are almost always satisfied.

**Time: 30 minutes to generate a full image set for any site**

---

### Stage 5: Write the Content With Claude (45 Minutes)

Most clients don't have website copy ready. Writing copy for them is an add-on service worth ₦15,000–₦40,000 per page — or you include it in your package.

Prompt for Claude:

> "Write the homepage copy for Lagos Dental Clinic. The clinic is in Lekki Phase 1, serves middle-class and upscale patients, and specialises in cosmetic and family dentistry. The tone should be professional but warm. Include: a headline, subheadline, 3 benefits, a brief about section, a services overview, and a call-to-action. Use ₦ for prices where appropriate."

Claude produces complete, professional copy that reads like it was written by a specialist copywriter.

**Time: 5–10 minutes per page of copy**

---

### Stage 6: Test and Deliver (1 Hour)

Before delivering to the client:

1. Test on mobile (open on your phone and check every page)
2. Test the contact form (submit a test enquiry)
3. Test the WhatsApp button (does it open WhatsApp with the right number?)
4. Check all images load properly
5. Check the site on both Chrome and Safari

Send the client a staging link. Walk them through it on a video call. Make any requested changes (usually minor at this stage).

Collect final payment. Transfer the domain or hosting access. Done.
