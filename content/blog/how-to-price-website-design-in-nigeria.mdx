Pricing is the number one thing Nigerian web designers get wrong. Most charge too little, work too hard, and burn out before they build a sustainable business.

This guide gives you a realistic framework for what to charge in 2026 — and how to hold your price when clients push back.

## The Market Reality

The Nigerian web design market has matured. Businesses that would have balked at ₦100,000 five years ago now understand that a website is a business asset, not a luxury.

That said, pricing varies enormously by:
- **Client type**: A street market vendor vs. a Victoria Island law firm are different markets entirely
- **Location**: Clients in Lekki, VI, and Ikoyi typically have bigger budgets than those in Oshodi or Alaba
- **Industry**: Fintech startups, clinics, and law firms spend more on digital than restaurants or fashion boutiques (though both need websites)

## Recommended Pricing Tiers for 2026

### Starter Site (3–5 pages)
**₦150,000 – ₦300,000**

- Who it's for: Small businesses, sole traders, salons, restaurants, local shops
- What's included: Home, About, Services, Contact, mobile-responsive design
- Timeline: 2–3 weeks
- Common additions: Google Maps embed, WhatsApp button, basic SEO

### Business Pro (6–10 pages)
**₦350,000 – ₦700,000**

- Who it's for: Established SMEs, law firms, clinics, agencies, hotels
- What's included: Portfolio/gallery, blog, team page, multiple service pages, SEO basics
- Timeline: 3–5 weeks
- Common additions: Booking form, staff profiles, testimonials section

### E-Commerce Site
**₦700,000 – ₦2,000,000+**

- Who it's for: Retail businesses, fashion brands, food delivery
- What's included: Product catalog, Paystack/Flutterwave integration, cart, order management
- Timeline: 5–8 weeks
- Note: Maintenance is especially important here — budget ₦30,000–₦80,000/month

### Custom Web Application
**₦1,500,000+**

- Who it's for: Startups, fintech, SaaS businesses, property platforms
- What's included: Database-backed, user auth, admin dashboards, API integrations
- Timeline: 8+ weeks
- This is a different service category entirely — price accordingly

## Why Maintenance Retainers Are Your Best Revenue

A ₦300,000 website project pays once. A ₦20,000/month maintenance retainer pays ₦240,000/year — for less work. Target 10 retainer clients and you have ₦2.4M recurring revenue before you take on a single new project.

What to include in a retainer:
- Monthly content updates
- Plugin/security updates
- Hosting management
- 2 hours of design changes per month
- Monthly traffic/SEO report

## How to Structure Payment

The standard approach that protects you:

- **50% deposit before you start** — non-negotiable. This filters time-wasters.
- **25% at mid-project review** — when the client approves the design
- **25% on delivery** — before you transfer the domain or give site access

Never deliver a finished website without full payment. Once a client has the site, leverage disappears.

## How to Justify Your Price

When clients say "that's expensive," they need to understand the value, not the cost.

**The ROI frame:**

> "A ₦250,000 website that brings in one extra client a month pays for itself in a single month. For a law firm billing ₦200,000/case, the site earns back its cost from the first new enquiry."

**The competitor frame:**

> "Your top three competitors in [area] all have websites. Every person who googles [service] in [area] and finds them instead of you is a client you're losing. That's the cost of not having one."

**The one-client test:**

> "If this website brings you just one new client in the next six months, does it pay for itself? For most businesses, yes. That's the baseline we're building toward."

## Stop Competing on Price

The biggest mistake Nigerian web designers make is racing to the bottom. There will always be someone willing to build a ₦30,000 website on a template. Don't compete there.

Position on quality, speed, and results. Find clients in industries where websites have high ROI — law, medicine, hospitality, professional services — and charge what those businesses can afford.

A single Lekki law firm client at ₦600,000 is worth more than twenty ₦30,000 Jumia-listing jobs.
