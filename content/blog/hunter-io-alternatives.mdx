The best Hunter.io alternative depends on why you're leaving: Apollo.io if you want a bigger contact database with built-in sequencing, Snov.io if you want something cheaper with similar core functionality, and Runvax if what you actually need isn't email-finding for existing companies but finding local businesses that don't have a website (and therefore aren't in Hunter's database at all).

Hunter.io does one thing well — finding and verifying email addresses tied to a known domain. People look for alternatives for three main reasons: price, database coverage, or because their actual target market (local SMBs) isn't well represented in domain-based search tools to begin with.

## Why People Leave Hunter.io

Hunter is a domain search tool at its core: give it a company website, it finds email patterns and verified addresses tied to that domain. That's exactly the problem with using it for local business prospecting — **if the business has no website, Hunter has nothing to search.** A significant share of small local businesses (restaurants, salons, auto shops, clinics) fall into exactly that gap. Hunter isn't doing anything wrong here; it's just not built for businesses without a domain.

The other common reasons people look for alternatives: Hunter's credit-based pricing ($49-299/mo depending on tier) adds up fast if you're doing high search volume, and it doesn't write outreach copy or manage a pipeline — it only finds the email, leaving the writing and tracking to you.

## Apollo.io: The Bigger-Database Alternative

If your complaint about Hunter is coverage or you want sequencing bundled in, Apollo is the natural upgrade path — a larger contact database (210M+ vs Hunter's domain-search approach), filtering by title/seniority/industry, and built-in email sequences so you're not exporting to a separate sender. It costs more (~$59/mo entry vs Hunter's ~$49/mo) but does more per subscription. It has the same fundamental limitation as Hunter for local no-website businesses, though — both are built around companies that already have a digital footprint to index.

## Snov.io: The Cheaper, Similar-Function Alternative

Snov.io is the closest like-for-like swap if price is your main issue — similar email-finding mechanics at a lower entry price (~$39/mo vs Hunter's ~$49/mo), plus basic drip campaigns bundled in that Hunter doesn't offer natively. It won't solve the local-business coverage gap either, since it pulls from the same category of scraped/database sources.

## Runvax: The Alternative for a Different Job Entirely

If the real issue is "Hunter comes back empty for the local businesses I'm trying to pitch," Apollo and Snov.io won't fix that — they share the same domain-dependent data limitation. Runvax solves a different problem: it searches live Google business listings by city and category, flags which businesses have no detectable website, and writes a personalized email or WhatsApp message for each one. There's no domain to search because the absence of a domain is the whole point of the lead.

This makes Runvax and Hunter genuinely complementary rather than pure substitutes in some workflows — agencies sometimes use Hunter for enterprise-side prospecting and Runvax for local SMB client acquisition simultaneously, because they're finding different kinds of leads.

## Which One to Actually Pick

- **Want more contacts and built-in sequencing:** Apollo.io.
- **Want to spend less for similar email-finding functionality:** Snov.io.
- **Your leads don't have websites/domains to search in the first place:** Runvax.
- **You need both enterprise contacts and local SMB leads:** run Hunter or Apollo alongside Runvax rather than choosing one.
