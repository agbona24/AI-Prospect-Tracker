There's no shortage of lead generation tools — the hard part is picking one that actually matches how you sell. A tool built for enterprise B2B sales teams solves a different problem than one built for a freelance web designer pitching local restaurants.

Here's a practical breakdown of the main categories, and where each one fits.

---

## The Categories That Actually Matter

Most "lead gen tools" fall into one of three buckets:

1. **B2B contact databases** (Apollo.io, ZoomInfo-style tools) — built to find email addresses and job titles at companies, mainly for enterprise/SaaS sales teams selling to other businesses' decision-makers.
2. **Email finder/verifier tools** (Hunter.io-style tools) — built specifically to find or verify a person's email address, not to source prospects in the first place.
3. **Local business prospecting tools** (like ProspectAI) — built to find local businesses by industry and city, and to flag a specific signal (like "no website") that tells you who's actually a warm lead.

If you sell to local businesses — web design, marketing services, signage, local SEO — category 3 matches your workflow far more closely than a generic B2B contact database.

---

## What a Generic B2B Database Gets Wrong for Local Prospecting

Tools built for enterprise sales are optimized for finding "VP of Marketing at a 500-person SaaS company." That's the wrong shape of data for someone selling websites to independent restaurants, salons, or law firms:

- They're priced and structured around volume of company records, not local business density
- They don't tell you which businesses are missing a website — the single strongest buying signal for a web design pitch
- They rarely include WhatsApp-friendly outreach, which matters heavily outside the US/UK market

---

## What to Look for in a Local Prospecting Tool

If your clients are local businesses, prioritize a tool that:

- **Searches by industry and city**, not just company size or job title
- **Flags a concrete buying signal** — like whether the business already has a website — so you're not cold-calling randomly
- **Generates personalized outreach automatically**, in whatever channel your market actually uses (email, WhatsApp, SMS)
- **Tracks your pipeline** from first contact through to won/lost, so nothing falls through the cracks

## Comparing the Trade-offs

| | B2B contact databases | Email finder tools | Local prospecting tools |
|---|---|---|---|
| Best for | Enterprise SaaS sales | Verifying a known contact's email | Local service businesses (web design, marketing, etc.) |
| Sourcing signal | Company size, job title | None — you supply the name | Industry + city + website presence |
| Outreach generation | Rarely included | Not included | Built-in, personalized per business |
| WhatsApp support | Rarely | No | Depends on tool — check before choosing |
| Local market fit (Africa, etc.) | Weak | Weak | Strong, if built for it |

---

## How to Actually Choose

Ask yourself one question: **who are you selling to?**

- Selling software to other companies' marketing or ops teams → a B2B contact database is the right tool.
- Just need to confirm someone's email address you already have a name for → an email finder/verifier is enough.
- Selling web design, local SEO, or marketing services to independent local businesses → a local prospecting tool built around industry + city + website-presence signals will save you the most time and convert the best, because it's solving the exact problem you have.
